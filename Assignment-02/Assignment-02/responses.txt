Data travels on the internet via bits that can travel up to millions of bits per second to relay information from one place to another.

IP (Internet protocol) is the computers individual address. DNS associates names like website URL's with their corresponding addresses (their IP).

A cookie is a small piece of data that is sent by a website and stored in the users computer. They are used to allow websites to identify users preferences and traits.

The three basic parts of a URL are the protocol, the domain name and the path.
