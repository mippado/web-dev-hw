Bitterroot

The Bitterroot is a true freestone river situated in between the Sapphire
and Bitterroot mountains in Southwest Montana. Beginning with the merging of
the East and West fork around Connor, MT, the 'Root flows through 80 miles
of gorgeous Bitterroot Valley before it's confluence with the Clark Fork river
in Missoula. The 'Root is famously known for it's world class dry fly fishing
that kicks off with the skwala stonefly hatch in March, and continues to
improve throughout the spring and summer with a variety of mayflies, stoneflies
and terrestrials that are ready to be eaten by the fish that inhabit this river.
The rapid development of the Bitterroot Valley allows anglers to find high class
amenities throughout the entire length of the river.
